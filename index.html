Our Story

Guardians of the Sungadi Heritage

✦

Thamayanthi Boutique is exclusively dedicated to preserving and promoting the living heritage of Chinalampatti Sungadi sarees and Madurai Sungudi sarees — two of Tamil Nadu's most celebrated GI-tagged handloom textiles. We work directly with master weavers from Chinalampatti (Dindigul district) and Madurai to bring you 100% authentic, certified handloom cotton sarees.

The Madurai Sungudi saree tradition traces back to the 16th century when Saurashtrian artisans settled in Madurai under the Nayak dynasty. Each tie-and-dye sungadi saree features over 20,000 individually hand-tied putta (dot) knots — then washed in Vaigai River water for a natural sheen found nowhere else. The Chinalampatti Sungadi today powers the economy of over 50,000 people in Dindigul and is exported to Singapore, Malaysia, Sri Lanka and South Africa.

About Chinalampatti Sungadi Sarees

✦

Chinalampatti Sungadi sarees originate from Chinalampatti village in Dindigul district, Tamil Nadu. These GI-tagged handloom sarees are crafted using the traditional tie-and-dye technique where each dot (putta) is individually hand-tied on pure cotton yarn before dyeing. The result is a vibrant, colour-fast pattern unique to each saree.

With over 30,000 weavers in the region, Chinalampatti is known as the "weaving village" of Tamil Nadu. The sarees are lightweight, breathable and perfect for the Tamil Nadu climate — making them ideal for daily wear, temple visits and festive occasions. They are exported globally and worn by Indian diaspora communities in Singapore, Malaysia and the UK.

About Madurai Sungudi Sarees

✦

Madurai Sungudi sarees are a 16th-century legacy textile from Madurai, Tamil Nadu, created by Saurashtrian weavers who migrated during the Nayak dynasty. Each saree features over 20,000 individually hand-tied putta (dot) knots. After the tie-dye process, the saree is washed in Vaigai River water — a step that gives Madurai Sungudi its distinctive natural sheen.

The Ayiram Butta variety features a thousand intricate dots with peacock (mayil) and rudraksha (chakram) motifs. The dual-dye bridal saree is worn by Saurashtrian brides and is one of the most sought-after wedding sarees in Tamil Nadu. Madurai Sungudi received its official Geographical Indication (GI) tag from the Government of India in December 2005.
